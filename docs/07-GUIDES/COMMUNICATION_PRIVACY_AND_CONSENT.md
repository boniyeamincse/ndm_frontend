# Communication Privacy And Consent Controls

## Goal

Define who may contact whom, what preferences members can manage, and how private communication data should be protected.

## Privacy Principles

- least-privilege access
- purpose-limited communication
- auditable high-sensitivity outreach
- no public exposure of private contact lists

## Allowed Contact Scope

- Central authority may contact broad member groups when organizationally required.
- District/campus leadership may contact only members within authorized units or assigned scopes.
- Direct member-to-member contact should not expose personal phone/email by default.

## Member Preferences

Members should be able to manage preferences for:

- announcement email
- urgent SMS alerts
- event reminders
- task or workflow notifications
- non-essential promotional communication

## Non-Optional Messages

The following may bypass opt-out where policy allows:

- account approval/rejection/suspension notices
- security alerts
- urgent organizational emergency alerts
- required governance communications

## Data Protection Rules

- store recipient contact data separately from public message bodies where possible
- do not expose recipient lists in frontend responses to non-privileged users
- redact contact details from exports unless explicitly authorized
- log mass communication sender, target scope, and delivery channel

## Sensitive Communications

- disciplinary or confidential notices should be visible only to intended recipients and authorized reviewers
- sensitive attachments require strict access checks and secure storage

## Abuse Prevention

- rate-limit bulk sends
- require elevated permission for all-member broadcasts
- record withdrawal and correction actions
- provide admin review for misuse of communication tools
